Goodbye Facebook..

"Why?" - "I'll miss you!" - "What about planning for the reunion? :("

That is just a small selection of the many comments I got, when I told my friends that I was quitting facebook. People couldn't really understand my motives, and to be honest, I'm not even sure as to why I, and yes I did do it, deleted my facebook account. This is me, trying to explain it, to you aswell as myself..

But why?

Let's be honest, facebook is a mixed bag; it's a practical platform for organizing events, coordinating with a study group, and a easy way to keep up with what your "friends" are doing.

But for some people, it gets addictive, endlessly just refreshing the facebook page, jumping in and out of it all the time - it's really bad.

I guess I was one of those people, I liked the likes a little too much, the instant dopamine fix had me running circles. Don't get me wrong, I wasn't THAT addicted, I was just addicted as I guess many people are. The difference for me, was that I found out.

Addicted_

I'm a frequent YouTube viewer which in my opinion is WAY better than browsing the endless meaninglessness of Facebook.

One day, I found a recorded livestream where Bryan Lunduke, a charming Linux enthusiast, sat down and straight up deleted his facebook account. Explaining all the great reasons he had to do so; Facebook was openly recording everything people did, said, browsed and liked, all the while made it addictive to use their site.

That had me thinking..

There was a study done, on the relation between the amount of "likes" a person gave to posts etc, and the individuals happiness, the study found that, the more you used facebook, the more likely you would be to be unhappy.

Ok, maybe it's not the whole picture, but get this; it's designed to be addictive, think about how you (how I) browse facebook, at some point you get numb, you feel like it's a waste of time. But every time you've been away from it for a while.. You instantly go back, in the need for some excitement, something to get the blood pumping.

Next time, when you see that red circle with the number in it, at the notification icon, think about how that makes you feel, repeat after me; I am an addict.

Life after facebook_

Now think about what you would do if you didn't have a facebook? Can you imagine your life without it - no right? That was for me the best reason to stop; the moment you truly believe that you can't live without something that redundant, seriously, IT'S FACEBOOK, it's hardly a basic human need.

That was when it hit me; I didn't want to quit facebook, I HAD TO!

It had me scared shitless; How would I keep up with birthdays? How would I make sure to still have friends after this, would they ever text me?

The truth is short and simple: The real friends will stick around, the rest will dissappear, not because you dislike each other or anything, but because your "friendship" was not real all along.

I don't have a grudge towards anybody but myself for this, believing that your facebook friends, somehow are your real friends, is believing in a lie, a big fat lie. In the end I was hoarder for facebook friends, I truly believed that having hundreds of superficial connections to people I hardly knew, somehow was going to make my life better.

But just like in the tv show "Hoarders", the things - in this case my "friends" just ended up getting in the way, weighing me down, they created so much static in my brain. Event's I should be interested in, group chats with a 100 notifications an hour. Vacation, wedding and party photos giving me FOMO, groups with bantering and internet language, bringing out the worst in civilized people.

Relieved_

Just after I deleted my account, one of my housemates had his 2nd child, he had posted it on facebook - I didn't see it. Later that day, one of my other housemates told me the news. That was when I got relieved; I wouldn't miss out on the important stuff.

I'm now on my.. well maybe 3rd day without facebook, and I'm perfectly happy, I don't know if I'm more or less productive. But there is one distinct difference, my life seems simpler, cleaner, it has less static.

And that is worth world to me. And I wouldn't trade it for a million likes.

\\ Jonas

Big data? Maybe?

What is big data? I still have an issue with truly grasping what it is. The term seems so inflated somehow, what makes data different from big data? Yes I get it, it's "bigger", but how much bigger? And is it nessesary for the data to be big, or does it just have to have a big impact?

HOME

There's a lot of questions to be answered, but I've recently been researching the topic. I've found a few good examples, like how the real estate agent HOME, used big data, for a clever marketing campaign. With data mined from other agents, they could know when to send potential customers, an offer to switch their realestate agent to home.
For more details, take a look at the video; It's quite clever!

Gas pickup points in India

This other one, talks about how they found all sorts of funny small data-caches and made them work together, to find out where the best locations for gas pick up points in India were.

I must say, WOW, think about what kind of a difference, one can make with just the right amount of Data! I'm just really excited!

All I can say is this, Big data is a big deal, it could be cool to learn even more about it.
But that might be for another time, later this year.

For now I'll be focusing on improving my CSS skills, and after that, Javascript!
